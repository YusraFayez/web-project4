INDIAN FOODS

8 top Indian restaurants in Riyadh:
Dive into dishes from every corner of the country

Are you ready to dig in at the best Indian restaurants in Riyadh?
You should be because the Saudi capital showcases a wide variety of dining experiences
from low-key budget-friendly spots to international fine-dining destinations that have come to the region for the first time.
Regardless of the price point or neighbourhood, the top Indian eateries celebrate a rich culinary history
showcase vibrant colours and flavours, and make your tastebuds zing.

Shah Rukh Khan

FOODS

All plush velvet furnishings, wide open spaces and grand chandeliers
that loom delicately over diners
Shah Rukh Khan offers a regal dining experience
in the centre of the Ar Rabi district.
Marrying cuisine from across India
China and Japan (and yes, that includes sushi)
it’s a hit for impeccable service,
fair prices and inventive dishes created with the perfect blend of spice.
Wash it all down with a couple of creatively crafted mocktails or blend of fresh teas for an extra treat.

Kumar

FOODS

Why choose between curry and tacos when you can have both at Kumar?
A lively yet laidback eatery from chef Kumar, who has spent the majority of his life working in the British restaurant industry,
dishes here are an innovative mash-up of modern and traditional recipes.
These include those aforementioned tacos that come filled with traditionally seasoned chicken, beef, lamb and vegetables.
Other highlights fuse Malaysian and Indian favourites including Maharaja tempura shrimps, Mughalai rib sliders and pistachio kulfi.

Babulal

FOODS

Can you take the spice? Babu Lal is one of the new restaurants in Riyadh
that opened from Jeddah and it’s all about the spice and aromas.
The Indian eatery serves authentic flavours, so if you like it extra spicy make sure to let it be known.
Cut yourself a slice of fresh naan straight out of the tanoor
and dunk it right into a fresh aromatic curry or a colourful tandoori chicken.

Mohalla

FOODS

Spice up your life at the new Mohalla restaurant.
Marking its second branch in Riyadh,
the Dubai-born Indian street food concept is cooking up classic street food dishes
known across Indian including signature dishes like raj kachori,
avocado papdi chaat and prawn curry.
The new branch is bigger than the first at The Esplanade with a seating capacity of 180.
Be prepared for flavour in a bite.

Amaly

FOODS

If you like your biriyani with a side of excellent views then we suggest booking a table at Amaly.
Home to a beautiful outdoor terrace where you can sit and watch the world
go by while dining on some of the best Indian food in Saudi.
This upscale eatery is a must-try for a special occasion with added spice.
Fusing high-end Indian and Middle Eastern cuisine, you can expect elevated staples
like Rogan josh lamb, chicken biryani and Punjabi-style samosas,
with heaps of veggie options thrown in for good measure.

Maharaja East by Vineet

FOODS

Celebrity chef Vineet Bhatia has restaurants dotted around the world,
including this popular spot in foodie fave As Sulimaniyah.
The menu is a riot of traditions, spice, local produce and classic-meets-modern cooking techniques.
The extensive menu has all your faves from curries to tandoor grills to biryanis.
Maharaja East by Vineet is a dependable, crowd-pleasing pick,
and the winner of Best Indian Restaurant in Riyadh for 2023.

Hara

FOODS

Take a trip to India at Hara. Located in Riyadh’s Park Avenue,
Hara offers classics like butter chicken,
tikka masala, chicken biryani, and mango and saffron lassi

RUHI

FOODS

Flavour in in dish. That’s what you’ll be getting with Indian cuisine.
The amount of spices and pungent aromas in Indian cooking with have you salivating from miles away.
Treat yourself to some spice for late-night restaurants in Riyadh with a bite.
RUHI serves classic Indian eats including puffy keema samosas and tikka masala.
